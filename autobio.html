Autobiography of Kibet Amos

Chapter 1: Early Beginnings

I was born on a cool, clear morning on May 7, 2000, in Kimugul village, nestled in the lush greenery of Bomet County, Bomet East Constituency. My parents, both dedicated and hardworking individuals, named me Kibet Amos. From my earliest days, they instilled in me the values of determination, honesty, and the importance of education—a foundation that would guide me throughout my life.

My childhood in Kimugul was painted with the vibrant colors of rural life. The rolling hills, the fertile farmlands, and the melodious songs of birds were the backdrop of my early years. I was always drawn to the natural world around me, and my curiosity about how things worked began to bloom in this rich environment. The simplicity of village life fostered a sense of wonder in me, sparking an early interest in understanding the mechanics of the world.

As a young boy, I was fascinated by the way things functioned. Whether it was a simple farm tool or an old, discarded piece of machinery, I wanted to understand its inner workings. My parents recognized this curiosity and encouraged it, often bringing home small gadgets for me to disassemble and explore. These early experiences ignited a passion for technology that would later shape my career.

One of my most vivid childhood memories is of the day when our village got its first television set. It was a black-and-white TV, and the entire village gathered around to watch the grainy images flicker on the screen. I was captivated—not just by the images themselves, but by the idea that a device could bring the world into our small village. From that moment on, I knew that I wanted to learn more about technology and how it could connect people and ideas..

Chapter 2: Primary Education - The Foundation Years

In 2006, at the age of six, I began my formal education at Kembu Primary School. The school, with its simple classrooms and expansive playground, became a place where my thirst for knowledge was nurtured. From the very first day, I was eager to learn, and I quickly developed a love for mathematics. Numbers seemed to speak to me, and I enjoyed the challenge of solving complex problems.

My enthusiasm for learning wasn't limited to the classroom. I was equally fascinated by the natural sciences, particularly when we studied electricity and machines. These subjects provided answers to many of the questions I had been pondering since childhood. Each lesson felt like a new piece of the puzzle, helping me understand the world around me a little better.

Outside the classroom, I continued to explore my interest in technology. Although our school didn’t have a computer lab, I made the most of the opportunities available to me. I would visit the local market, where a small cyber cafe had recently opened, just to catch a glimpse of the computers inside. The idea of a machine that could perform calculations, store information, and even connect to other machines across the world was incredibly exciting to me.

As I progressed through primary school, my academic performance consistently placed me among the top students. I enjoyed the respect of my peers and teachers, but I remained grounded, always willing to help others with their studies. In my final year of primary school, I was appointed as the class monitor, a role that taught me valuable lessons in leadership and responsibility

The culmination of my primary education came in 2014 with the Kenya Certificate of Primary Education (KCPE) exams. I approached these exams with a mix of confidence and anxiety, knowing that my performance would shape my future. When the results were announced, I was thrilled to learn that I had scored 362 marks, earning a B+ grade. This achievement was a testament to the hard work I had put in, and it filled me with hope for the next stage of my educational journey.

Chapter 3: Secondary Education - Expanding Horizons

In 2011, I stepped through the gates of St. Anthony Boys High School in Kitale, ready to embark on the next phase of my academic journey. The transition from primary to secondary school was both exciting and challenging. The campus was larger, the coursework more demanding, and the competition fiercer. However, I was determined to build upon the foundation I had laid in primary school. From the outset, I immersed myself in the rich academic environment of St. Anthony's. The school's motto, "Vitam Impendere Vero" (To Devote One's Life to Truth), resonated deeply with me. I saw each class, each assignment, as an opportunity to uncover new truths about the world around me.

My passion for technology continued to grow during these years. The school had a well-equipped computer lab, and I spent countless hours there, expanding my knowledge of various software applications and diving deeper into the world of programming. It was during this time that I wrote my first complete program - a simple calculator application. Seeing the lines of code I had written come to life on the screen was a thrilling experience, one that cemented my desire to pursue a career in Information Technology.

In my final year at St. Anthony's, I was faced with the crucial task of selecting my subjects for the Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education (KCSE) exams. Without hesitation, I chose Mathematics, Physics, and Computer Studies as my core subjects. These choices reflected my passion for technology and my desire to build a strong foundation for a future career in IT. The final year of secondary school was intense, filled with long hours of study and preparation for the KCSE exams. I approached these exams with the same determination and focus that had characterized my entire academic journey. When the results were announced in 2014, I was elated to learn that I had achieved an A- grade with 79 points

This achievement was more than just a personal victory. It was a stepping stone towards my dreams, opening doors to higher education and bringing me one step closer to a career in Information Technology. As I bid farewell to St. Anthony's, I felt a mix of nostalgia for the memories I had made and excitement for the future that lay ahead. Little did I know that the next chapter of my life would be even more transformative, shaping me into the IT professional I am today.

Chapter 4: University Years - Specializing in Information Technology

In 2015, I joined Kaplong Boys High School in Bomet County, eager to embark on the next chapter of my education. The transition to secondary school was both exhilarating and challenging. The school was much larger than my primary school, and the coursework was more demanding. However, I was determined to excel and make the most of the opportunities before me.

At Kaplong, I continued to nurture my passion for technology. The school had a well-equipped computer lab, and I spent countless hours there, exploring various software applications and learning the basics of programming. It was during this time that I wrote my first simple program—a basic calculator application. The experience of seeing my code come to life on the screen was thrilling, and it deepened my desire to pursue a career in technology.

My academic journey in secondary school was marked by a deepening interest in the sciences. I was particularly drawn to physics, which seemed to provide the answers to many of the questions I had about how the world worked. Mathematics, too, remained a favorite subject, and I often stayed after class to work on additional problems with my teachers.

In my final year at Kaplong Boys High School, I faced the important task of selecting my subjects for the Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education (KCSE) exams. Without hesitation, I chose Mathematics, Physics, and Computer Studies as my core subjects. These choices reflected my growing interest in technology and my desire to build a strong foundation for a future career in the field.

The final year of secondary school was intense, filled with long hours of study and preparation for the KCSE exams. When the results were announced in 2018, I was elated to learn that I had achieved a B- grade with 59 points. This accomplishment was not only a personal victory but also a crucial step toward realizing my dreams.

Chapter 5: Early Career and Professional Development

As I approached the end of my university education, I was eager to put my skills to the test in the professional world. My first opportunity came in 2015 when I was offered a position as a cyber manager at Rimaya Cyber in Bungoma. Despite still being a student, I jumped at the chance to gain real-world experience. Managing the cyber cafe was a crash course in customer service, technical support, and business operations. I was responsible for maintaining the computers, troubleshooting network issues, and ensuring a smooth experience for our customers. This role taught me the importance of clear communication, especially when explaining technical concepts to non-technical users. After completing my degree, I continued to seek out opportunities to expand my skill set. In 2017 and 2018, I worked as a Microsoft Office trainer, helping individuals and small businesses improve their productivity through better use of these essential tools. This experience honed my teaching skills and deepened my understanding of office productivity software.

The year 2020 brought a significant opportunity when I secured a position as an ICT Technical Support specialist at the Nairobi Centre for International Arbitration. This role exposed me to a more corporate environment and allowed me to work with more advanced systems. I was responsible for maintaining the center's IT infrastructure, providing technical support to staff, and ensuring the smooth operation of technology during arbitration proceedings. Working at the arbitration center was a turning point in my career. It exposed me to the critical role of IT in facilitating important legal processes and taught me the importance of maintaining system reliability and data confidentiality in sensitive environments.

Throughout these early career experiences, I continued to invest in my professional development. I completed additional certifications, including courses in Internet of Things, Networking Basics, and Introduction to Cybersecurity. Each new certification expanded my knowledge base and made me a more versatile IT professional. In 2021, I took on a role that broadened my technical expertise even further. I joined Otomart Supermarket Mutalia, where I was responsible for the maintenance, installation, and sales of electronics. This position allowed me to work with a wide range of electronic devices, from point-of-sale systems to security cameras. It deepened my understanding of hardware and reinforced my troubleshooting skills. These diverse experiences in my early career have shaped me into a well-rounded IT professional. From managing a cyber cafe to supporting high-stakes arbitration proceedings, from designing websites to maintaining complex electronic systems, each role has added a new dimension to my skill set and strengthened my problem-solving abilities.

Chapter 6: Skills and Expertise

In 2019, with my KCSE results in hand and a clear vision for my future, I enrolled at Dedan Kimathi University of Technology to pursue a Bachelor of Science in Information Technology. The campus, with its modern facilities and vibrant academic community, felt like a place where my dreams could truly take shape. From the very first lecture, I was captivated by the world of C+ programming, computer essentials, and calculus.

The curriculum at Dedan Kimathi University was rigorous and comprehensive. I found myself immersed in a world of coding, where I learned not only the fundamentals of programming but also how to think critically and solve complex problems. The university’s computer labs became my second home, and I spent countless nights coding and experimenting with new technologies.

Chapter 7: Personal Interests and Future Aspirations

One of my most fulfilling activities outside of work is volunteering. I believe strongly in giving back to the community that has supported me throughout my journey. I regularly participate in local initiatives aimed at introducing young people to technology. Once a month, I conduct free basic computer literacy classes at the community center where I first encountered a computer. Seeing the spark of excitement in the eyes of these young learners reminds me of my own childhood and reinforces my commitment to spreading technological knowledge.

Reading has always been a passion of mine, serving as both a source of knowledge and relaxation. I particularly enjoy books on emerging technologies, biographies of tech innovators, and science fiction that explores the potential future impacts of technology on society. Some of my favorite authors include Isaac Asimov for his forward-thinking approach to technology in fiction, and Walter Isaacson for his insightful biographies of tech pioneers. In recent years, I've taken up blogging as a way to share my knowledge and insights with a wider audience. My blog, "Tech Horizons with Iyaya," covers various topics in IT, from beginner-friendly tutorials to discussions on the latest trends in cybersecurity and artificial intelligence. Through this platform, I've connected with tech enthusiasts from around the world, engaging in stimulating discussions and continually broadening my perspectives.

In conclusion, my life's path has been shaped by a deep passion for technology, a commitment to continuous learning, and a desire to use my skills for the betterment of society. As I move forward, I remain dedicated to pushing the boundaries of what's possible in IT, always with an eye towards how technology can be leveraged to create a more connected, efficient, and equitable world. The journey that began with dismantling old radios as a child has led me to a life filled with purpose, challenges, and the endless possibilities that technology offers. I look forward to the next chapters of this exciting adventure in the world of Information Technology.
